# Dominion Complete Rules Reference

*Distilled from Dominion Complete Rules v11.1 (2025, Nick Knutsen). Covers base game + all 16 expansions.*

---

## 1. Game Overview

Dominion is a deck-building card game for 2-6 players. Each player starts with an identical 10-card deck (7 Coppers + 3 Estates) and builds their deck during the game by buying cards from a shared Supply. The player with the most Victory Points (VP) at the end wins.

### Core Loop
1. Draw 5 cards from your deck
2. On your turn: play Actions, play Treasures, buy cards
3. Discard everything (hand + play area), draw 5 new cards
4. When your draw pile is empty and you need cards, shuffle your discard pile to form a new draw pile

---

## 2. Turn Structure

A turn has three main phases, executed in order:

### A - Action Phase
- You start with **1 Action** in your Action pool
- You may play one Action card from your hand, which consumes 1 Action from your pool
- Action cards may grant "+X Actions", adding to your Action pool
- You may keep playing Action cards as long as Actions remain in your pool
- You may choose to stop playing Actions at any time (even with Actions remaining)

### B - Buy Phase
**First part:** Play Treasures
- Play as many Treasure cards as you want from your hand, in any order
- Each Treasure adds Coins to your money pool
- You cannot play more Treasures after buying a card

**Second part:** Buy cards
- You start with **1 Buy** in your Buy pool
- You may buy a card from the Supply by paying its cost (deducting from money pool) and using 1 Buy
- The bought card goes to your discard pile (this is "gaining" it)
- You may buy Events or Projects instead of cards (still uses 1 Buy and costs money)
- You may keep buying as long as you have Buys and money remaining
- You can buy any Supply card you can afford, including Curses

### C - Clean-up Phase
- Discard all cards in play and all cards remaining in hand
- Draw 5 new cards from your deck
- If deck has fewer than 5 cards, shuffle discard pile into deck, then continue drawing

### Night Phase (Nocturne expansion)
- Between Buy phase and Clean-up phase
- Play as many Night cards as you want from hand

### Resource Pools
Each turn you track three pools:
- **Action pool**: starts at 1, cards add to it via "+X Actions"
- **Buy pool**: starts at 1, cards add to it via "+X Buys"
- **Money pool**: starts at 0, Treasures and Action cards add to it via "+X Coins"

Pool contents persist until end of turn. Coins produced during Action phase can be spent in Buy phase.

## 3. Card Types

### Primary Types (determined by frame color)
| Type | Frame Color | Description |
|------|------------|-------------|
| Action | White | Played in Action phase. Has a play ability. |
| Treasure | Yellow | Played in Buy phase. Produces Coins. |
| Victory | Green | Worth VP at end of game. Usually no play ability. |
| Curse | Purple | Worth -1 VP. No play ability. |
| Reaction | Blue | Has an ability usable at specific times (even outside your turn). |
| Duration | Orange | Stays in play beyond current turn; has future effects. |
| Night | Black | Played in Night phase (after Buy, before Clean-up). |
| Reserve | Tan | Goes to Tavern mat when played. Can be "called" later. |
| Shadow | — | Can be played from your deck as if from hand. |
| Ruins | Brown | Weak Action cards (like Curses but type Action). |

### Secondary/Modifier Types
Attack, Doom, Fate, Heirloom, Liaison, Looter, Omen, Shelter, Spirit, Zombie, Traveller, Command, Gathering, Augur, Castle, Clash, Fort, Knight, Loot, Odyssey, Prize, Reward, Townsfolk, Wizard

Cards can have multiple types (e.g., "Action - Attack", "Action - Reaction", "Treasure - Victory").

## 4. Card Anatomy

- **Name**: top of card
- **Cost**: lower-left corner (in Coins; some include Potion or Debt)
- **Types**: bottom of card
- **Abilities**: center text
  - **Play ability**: above the dividing line - resolved when card is played
  - **Other abilities**: below the dividing line - triggered at specific times (when-gain, when-trash, when-discard, etc.)

## 5. Locations in the Game

### Supply
- Cards available to be bought/gained
- Contains: basic Treasures (Copper, Silver, Gold), basic Victory cards (Estate, Duchy, Province), Curses, and 10 Kingdom card piles
- Only the **top card** of each pile is available to buy/gain
- With Prosperity: may also include Platinum, Colony
- With Alchemy: may include Potion

### Player Zones
- **Deck**: face-down draw pile. You draw from the top.
- **Discard pile**: face-up pile of discarded cards. Only the top card is visible to opponents.
- **Hand**: cards you currently hold (private information)
- **Play area** ("in play"): where you put cards you play. Cards stay here until Clean-up.
- **Set aside**: cards placed outside your play area (e.g., on mats)

### Shared Zones
- **Trash pile**: face-up, visible to all. Cards here belong to no player and are not part of Supply.
- **Non-Supply piles**: e.g., Prizes, Spoils, Horses, Loot (gained only through specific card effects)

### Ownership
Your cards = cards in your deck, hand, discard pile, play area, or set aside by you.

### Open Information
**Visible to all players:**
- All cards in trash
- Number of cards in all Supply and non-Supply piles
- All cards in each player's play area
- All face-up set-aside cards
- Number of cards in each player's hand
- Top card of each player's discard pile
- Number of face-down set-aside cards
- All tokens

**Visible only to you:**
- Cards in your hand
- Face-down set-aside cards
- Number of cards in your deck

## 6. Game Setup

### Basic Setup (every game)
1. Each player takes 7 Coppers + 3 Estates as their starting deck
2. Place in Supply:
   - **Copper**: 60 minus those dealt to players
   - **Silver**: 40
   - **Gold**: 30
   - **Estate**: 12 (8 in 2-player)
   - **Duchy**: 12 (8 in 2-player)
   - **Province**: 12 (8 in 2-player)
   - **Curse**: 10 per player beyond the first (10 in 2p, 20 in 3p, 30 in 4p)
   - **10 Kingdom card piles**: 10 cards each (Victory Kingdom piles: 12 cards, or 8 in 2-player)
3. Place Trash mat/card
4. Randomly choose starting player; play proceeds clockwise
5. Each player shuffles their deck and draws 5 cards

### Player Count Adjustments
- **2 players**: 8 cards in all Victory piles (including Kingdom Victory piles)
- **5-6 players**: 15 or 18 Provinces (add 3 per player beyond 4); use extra Treasures; game ends when **4** Supply piles are empty (not 3)

### Expansion Setup Rules
- **Prosperity**: Randomly include Colonies (12) + Platinums (12) based on proportion of Prosperity Kingdom cards
- **Dark Ages**: Randomly replace Estates with Shelters based on proportion of Dark Ages cards
- **Alchemy**: If any Kingdom card has Potion in cost, include 16 Potions
- **Nocturne**: Heirloom cards replace one Copper in starting deck; Boon/Hex decks if Fate/Doom cards present
- **Allies**: If Liaison cards present, include 1 Ally; each player gets Favors mat + 1 Favor token
- **Plunder**: If Traits included, assign randomly to Treasure or Action Kingdom piles
- **Rising Sun**: If Omen cards present, include 1 Prophecy with Sun tokens (5/8/10/12/13 for 2/3/4/5/6 players)

## 7. Game End and Scoring

### Game End Conditions
The game ends at the end of a player's turn when **either**:
1. The Province pile is empty, **OR**
2. Any **3** Supply piles are empty (4 piles for 5-6 players)

With Colonies: game also ends when Colony pile is empty.

### Scoring
1. Gather all your cards (deck, hand, discard, play area, set aside)
2. Count VP from:
   - Victory cards (Estate=1, Duchy=3, Province=6, Colony=10)
   - Curse cards (-1 each)
   - VP tokens
   - Kingdom Victory cards (Gardens, etc.)
   - Landmarks and Allies that apply
3. Player with most VP wins
4. **Tiebreaker**: fewer turns played wins. If still tied, shared victory.

## 8. Playing a Card

When you play a card (Action, Treasure, or Night):
1. Announce it and place it in your play area (face up)
2. Follow instructions from top to bottom, stopping at dividing line
3. Resolve all effects of the play ability

### Key Rules
- A card is considered "played" as soon as it's announced (before resolving)
- Cards stay in play until Clean-up (unless they're Duration cards)
- You can play a card even if you can't do everything it says - do as much as you can
- "+1 Action" means add 1 to your Action pool, NOT play an Action immediately
- "+1 Coin" means add 1 to your money pool, NOT gain a Copper

---

## 9. Gaining Cards

- **Buying** a card results in **gaining** it
- When you gain a card, take it from the Supply and place it face up in your discard pile (unless otherwise specified)
- You can only gain cards that are available (in Supply or as specified by effects)
- Cards from non-Supply piles can only be gained by effects that specifically say to
- When instructed to "gain a card costing up to X", you must choose an available card if one exists in that cost range

### Gain to Other Locations
Some effects gain cards directly to hand, deck (top), or other locations. The card skips the discard pile entirely. When-gain abilities still trigger afterward.

## 10. Core Terminology (Card Text)

| Term | Meaning |
|------|---------|
| +X Actions | Add X to your Action pool |
| +X Cards / Draw X | Draw X cards from deck to hand |
| +X Buys | Add X to your Buy pool |
| +X Coins (or +$X) | Add X Coins to your money pool |
| Discard | Move card(s) from hand to discard pile (unless stated otherwise) |
| Trash | Remove card(s) from the game (place in trash pile) |
| Gain | Take a card and put it in your discard pile (usually from Supply) |
| Reveal | Show card(s) to all players |
| Look at | Look at card(s) privately (don't show other players) |
| Set aside | Place card face up outside your play area |
| "may" | Optional effect |
| "if you do/did" | Contingent on actually doing the previous effect |
| "do X to" / "do X for" | Same as "if you do" - second effect contingent on first |
| "each other player" | All players except the current player |
| "any number" | Includes zero |
| Name a card | Say any card name (even cards not in the game) |

## 11. Timing Rules

### Ability Trigger Types
- **Play ability**: resolved when card is played (above dividing line)
- **Before-play**: triggers when another card is played, before it resolves (Reactions to Attacks, Kiln, Urchin)
- **After-play**: triggers after a played card is resolved (Royal Carriage, Citadel, Flagship)
- **When-buy**: triggers when you buy a card, after paying but before gaining
- **When-gain**: triggers when you gain a card, after it reaches its destination
- **When-discard**: triggers when you discard a card from play (or hand for certain cards)
- **When-trash**: triggers when you trash a card, after it reaches the trash
- **Start-of-turn**: triggers at the start of your Action phase
- **While-in-play (ongoing)**: active as long as the card is in play

### Concurrent Abilities
1. When an effect affects multiple players simultaneously, resolve in turn order starting with current player
2. When a player has multiple concurrent abilities to resolve, they choose the order
3. When multiple abilities trigger at the same time across players, current player resolves first, then each player in turn order

### "Lose Track" Rule
- An ability that refers to a card expects it to be in a certain location
- If another ability moves the card first, the original ability **loses track** of it and cannot move it
- A play ability expects the card to be in play
- A when-gain ability expects the card to be where the gaining effect put it
- A when-discard ability expects the card to be in the discard pile
- An ability that has lost track of a card cannot move or play it (exception: throne-rooms can replay cards they lost track of per 2021 errata)

### Resolving Multiple Abilities
- If resolving an ability triggers another ability, resolve the new one immediately before continuing the first
- "Do X and Y" or "do X twice" means resolve each part separately; abilities can trigger between parts
- Must resolve all triggered abilities before moving to the next phase

---

## 12. Reaction Cards

- Have an ability that can be resolved at specific times (described on the card)
- Resolving a Reaction does NOT count as playing the card (unless the ability says to play it)
- Most common: react when another player plays an Attack
  - Reactions to Attacks resolve BEFORE the Attack resolves (you react before the Attack affects you)
  - Moat and Shield are the only Reactions that fully nullify an Attack's effect on you
  - Other Reactions (like Diplomat, Secret Chamber) just give you their Reaction ability
- You can react multiple times to the same occurrence (if the Reaction resolves from hand)
- Multiple players react in turn order, starting with the current player
- After any player resolves any ability, all players get another chance to react

---

## 13. Duration Cards

- When played, set up a future ability (typically at start of your next turn)
- Stay in play instead of being discarded during Clean-up IF they have unresolved future abilities
- Discarded during Clean-up of the turn their last ability resolves
- If a throne-room plays a Duration, the throne-room also stays in play until the Duration is discarded
- If a Duration fails to set up a future ability (e.g., no cards set aside), it IS discarded in Clean-up
- 2025 rule: if a Duration's play ability loses track of it (card leaves play), future abilities stop after current turn

## 16. Cost System

### Basic Cost
- Most cards cost only Coins (0-11 range in base + Prosperity)
- Costs can't go below 0

### Special Cost Components
- **Potion** (Alchemy): Some cards cost Coins + Potion (e.g., 2P = 2 Coins + 1 Potion). Must play a Potion Treasure to produce Potion.
- **Debt** (Empires, Rising Sun): Some cards cost Debt tokens instead of/alongside Coins. Take Debt tokens when buying; can't buy anything until all Debt is paid off (1 Coin = 1 Debt repaid).

### Cost Comparison Rules
- A cost of {3 Coins, 1 Potion} is NOT comparable to {4 Coins, 0 Potion} (neither is lower)
- "Costing up to X" means each component (Coins, Potion, Debt) must be <= X's components
- "Costing exactly X more" means same Potion/Debt amounts, just X more Coins
- Cost reduction (e.g., Bridge: "all cards cost 1 less") only affects the Coin component
- Cost reduction applies to ALL cards in the game (Supply, players' cards, trash), not just buyable cards
- Event and Project costs cannot be reduced

---

## 17. Shuffling Rules

- Only shuffle your discard pile when you need to draw/reveal/look at cards from your deck AND your deck is empty
- Don't shuffle preemptively
- When shuffling: take all cards from discard pile, shuffle, place as new deck
- If you need X cards and after shuffling there still aren't enough, just take what you can
- When revealing cards from deck and you need to shuffle midway, keep revealed cards aside (don't shuffle them back in)

## 18. Expansion Mechanics Summary

### Duration Cards (Seaside, Adventures, Empires, Nocturne, Renaissance, Menagerie, Allies, Plunder, Rising Sun)
Cards that stay in play and have effects on future turns. See Section 13.

### Reserve Cards (Adventures)
- Placed on Tavern mat when played
- Can be "called" (moved to play area) at specific times described on card
- Calling is NOT playing - doesn't use Actions, doesn't trigger before-play/after-play abilities
- Discarded from play in that turn's Clean-up

### Night Cards (Nocturne)
- Played in Night phase (after Buy, before Clean-up)
- Play as many as you want, no Action cost
- Not Action cards - "Action" related abilities don't apply to them

### Shadow Cards (Rising Sun)
- Have unique backs; placed at bottom of deck after shuffling
- Whenever you could play a card from hand, you may play a Shadow card from your deck instead

### Events (Adventures, Empires, Menagerie, Plunder, Rising Sun)
- Not cards you gain; abilities you buy in Buy phase
- Cost is in upper-left corner
- Uses 1 Buy and Coins from money pool
- Buying an Event is NOT buying a card (doesn't trigger when-buy-a-card effects)

### Projects (Renaissance)
- Ongoing abilities you buy in Buy phase
- Place a Project cube on it; ability active for rest of game
- Max 2 Projects per player per game

### Landmarks (Empires)
- Always active for all players
- Give alternate VP scoring or VP tokens

### Allies (Allies)
- Always active for all players
- Abilities powered by spending Favor tokens
- Included when Liaison-type Kingdom cards are present

### Ways (Menagerie)
- Available to all players whenever an Action is played
- When playing an Action, you may resolve the Way instead of the Action's play ability
- Only replaces the play ability (above dividing line); other abilities still function
- A Duration played using a Way doesn't set anything up, so it's discarded in Clean-up

### Traits (Plunder)
- Assigned to a specific Kingdom pile during setup
- Always active, applying to cards from that pile

### Prophecies (Rising Sun)
- Start inactive with Sun tokens
- Each time a player resolves "+Sun" on an Omen card, remove a Sun token
- When last token removed, Prophecy activates for all players for rest of game

### Boons and Hexes (Nocturne)
- Drawn from shuffled Boon/Hex decks
- Boons: positive effects (received by Fate-type cards)
- Hexes: negative effects (inflicted by Doom-type cards)

### Coffers and Villagers (Guilds, Renaissance)
- **Coffers**: tokens stored on your mat. Spend any time during your turn for +1 Coin each.
- **Villagers**: tokens stored on your mat. Spend during your Action phase for +1 Action each.
- Both can be spent in the middle of resolving an ability.

### Favors (Allies)
- Tokens stored on Favors mat
- Spent as described by the Ally card in the game

### Exile (Menagerie)
- Exile mat: place cards here via "Exile" effects
- When you gain a card with the same name as a card on your Exile mat, you may discard all copies of that name from Exile
- Cards on Exile mat are yours but Exiling is not gaining

### VP Tokens (Prosperity, Empires)
- Physical tokens worth VP at end of game
- Gained through card effects; can never be lost once gained

### Debt (Empires, Rising Sun)
- Some cards/Events cost Debt tokens
- Take Debt tokens when buying; can't buy anything while in Debt
- Pay off Debt any time during your turn (1 Coin per Debt token)

### Overpaying (Guilds, Cornucopia & Guilds)
- Cards with "+" after cost allow overpaying
- Pay more than the cost when buying; get an effect based on how much extra you paid
- Can overpay with Potions but not with Debt

### Exchanging (Adventures, Nocturne)
- Return a card to its pile and take a different card from that pile
- NOT considered gaining
- Used mainly by Traveller upgrade lines

### Split Piles (Dark Ages, Empires, Allies)
- Single pile containing differently named cards
- Only top card is available to buy/gain
- Types: Ruins, Knights (Dark Ages); five 5/5 split piles (Empires); six 4x4 Augur/Clash/Fort/Odyssey/Townsfolk/Wizard piles (Allies)
- "Rotate" (Allies): move all identical top cards to bottom, revealing next card type

## 19. Key Strategic Concepts (for AI)

### Card Categories by Function
- **Village**: provides +2 or more Actions (Village, Port, Festival, etc.) - enables playing multiple Action cards
- **Smithy/Draw**: provides +3 or more Cards (Smithy, Laboratory, etc.) - increases hand quality
- **Trasher**: removes weak cards from deck (Chapel, Remodel, etc.) - improves deck consistency
- **Gainer**: gains cards without buying (Workshop, Artisan, etc.) - accelerates deck building
- **Attack**: harms other players (Militia, Witch, etc.) - slows opponents
- **+Buy**: allows buying multiple cards per turn (Market, Festival, etc.)

### Fundamental Strategy Principles
- **Deck thinning**: Removing starting Coppers/Estates increases average hand quality
- **Engine building**: Combining Villages + draw cards to play many Actions per turn
- **Big Money**: Buying mostly Treasures and Provinces (Silver/Gold rush)
- **Terminal collision**: Having multiple terminal Actions (no +Action) risks dead draws
- **Greening**: Timing when to start buying Victory cards (they clog your deck)
- **Pile control**: Monitoring when piles will empty to trigger/prevent game end

### VP Density
- Province: 6 VP for 8 cost = 0.75 VP/coin
- Duchy: 3 VP for 5 cost = 0.6 VP/coin
- Estate: 1 VP for 2 cost = 0.5 VP/coin
- Colony: 10 VP for 11 cost = 0.91 VP/coin
- Curse: -1 VP (penalty)

## 20. Important Rule Clarifications

### Effects Are Immediate
- When you resolve "+2 Actions", you add them to your pool immediately
- A Mining Village gives +2 Actions even if you trash it for +2 Coins
- A Copper produces +1 Coin even if it's trashed by a Counterfeit

### Do As Much As You Can
- You may play a card even if you can't do everything it says
- You must do as much as possible (if not optional)
- You may choose any option, even one you can't fully carry out
- If an effect refers to a card that doesn't exist, that effect fails

### "If You Do" Is Contingent
- "Trash a card. If you did, gain a card costing up to 2 more" - if you can't trash, you don't gain
- "Discard 2 cards for +2 Coins" - if you don't discard 2, you get nothing

### Buying vs Gaining
- Buying always results in gaining, but gaining doesn't require buying
- "When you buy" triggers are separate from "when you gain" triggers
- When you buy a card: pay cost -> when-buy triggers -> gain the card -> when-gain triggers

### Cards "In Your Deck"
- When an ability says "cards in your deck", it means ALL your cards (deck + hand + discard + play area + set aside)
- This is mainly relevant for end-of-game scoring (Gardens, etc.)

### The Supply
- Only Supply cards can be bought
- Non-Supply piles (Prizes, Spoils, Horses, etc.) can only be gained by specific effects
- Empty Supply piles still count as piles (for game end, Poacher, etc.)

## 21. Common Mistakes to Avoid (Engine Implementation)

1. **Curses are included in every game** (even without Attack cards)
2. When you play a card, it stays in play until Clean-up - don't move it to discard immediately
3. You must PLAY Treasures to produce Coins - they don't produce Coins automatically from hand
4. Don't discard cards from hand just because you can't play them - wait for Clean-up
5. Don't shuffle your discard pile until you actually need a card from an empty deck
6. +1 Action adds to the Action pool - it doesn't mean "play another Action immediately"
7. +1 Coin adds to money pool - it doesn't mean "gain a Copper"
8. You play Treasures first, then buy. You can't buy a card and then play more Treasures.
9. Buying a card usually results in gaining it to your discard pile (not hand, not deck)
10. When drawing and your deck runs out mid-draw, shuffle discard pile, then continue drawing. Don't shuffle in remaining cards.
11. Throne Room + Action costs only 1 Action (not 2) from your Action pool
12. When an effect tells you to gain multiple cards, resolve each gain separately with its when-gain triggers
